Plate Heat Exchanger

Efficient thermal energy exchange at the intersection of engineering and innovation

What is a Plate Heat Exchanger?

Core Function

A plate heat exchanger (PHE) is a device designed to transfer heat between two fluids with minimal contact. It consists of a series of thin corrugated metal plates that facilitate efficient thermal energy exchange.

How It Works

Hot and cold fluids flow alternately between corrugated metal plates. The thin walls and turbulent flow pattern maximize heat transfer efficiency, enabling rapid temperature equilibration between the two fluid streams.

Efficiency Factor

PHEs achieve thermal efficiencies of 80-95%, significantly higher than traditional shell-and-tube designs. The compact design allows for high heat transfer rates with minimal pressure drop.

Historical Evolution

Plate heat exchangers were first introduced in the 1930s by ALFA-LAVAL, revolutionizing thermal management in industrial processes. Since then, they've become the standard choice across numerous industries due to their superior efficiency, compact size, and reliability.
